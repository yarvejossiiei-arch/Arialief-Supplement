### Palmitoylethanolamide (PEA) — The Standout Ingredient

Arialief incorporates Palmitoylethanolamide (PEA), a naturally occurring fatty acid amide with potent anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective properties. PEA works by binding to receptors in the nervous system that control inflammation and pain signaling. It does not create dependency or sedation, which is one reason it has drawn significant attention in neuropathy research circles. The brand refers to PEA under the name Primepalm™, describing it as the signature ingredient that targets sciatic nerve pain by calming inflammation and protecting nerve pathways.

### Alpha Lipoic Acid (ALA) — The Most Studied Nerve Antioxidant

Alpha lipoic acid is the ingredient with the deepest body of clinical research behind it. It is a potent, dual-purpose antioxidant — meaning it works in both water-soluble and fat-soluble environments in the body, which is relatively rare among antioxidants.

A 2024 double-blind, placebo-controlled study published in Cureus evaluated ALA at 600 mg daily in patients with diabetic peripheral neuropathy and found it targets oxidative stress by neutralizing reactive oxygen species (ROS) that cause damage to neuronal cells and worsen neuropathic symptoms. ALA has been recommended in the 2022 guidelines of the International Diabetes Federation as a treatment option for patients who are intolerant of first-line pharmacotherapies.

The most studied effective daily dose in clinical trials sits between 600 mg and 1,200 mg per day. Arialief does not publicly disclose exact per-ingredient dosages on its label in all markets — a transparency point worth noting.

### Magnesium Glycinate — The Nerve Calmer

Magnesium Glycinate is known for its high bioavailability and is included in Arialief for its role in nerve conduction, muscle relaxation, and helping reduce cramps and spasms that often accompany neuropathy. What makes magnesium glycinate stand out from other magnesium forms is its binding to the amino acid glycine — boosting absorption while being easier on the digestive system. Research from the National Institutes of Health (NIH) confirms that magnesium plays a direct role in modulating NMDA receptors, the very pain receptors that become overactive in chronic nerve pain conditions.

### Butcher's Broom (Ruscus aculeatus) — The Circulation Booster

Poor blood flow to peripheral nerves is one of the main reasons neuropathy worsens over time, especially for people with diabetes or metabolic issues. Butcher's Broom improves blood circulation, ensuring vital nutrients and oxygen reach nerve cells for optimal health. The active compounds in butcher's broom, known as ruscogenins, have been studied for their ability to tone blood vessel walls and reduce venous pooling — the sluggish circulation that starves peripheral nerves of the nutrients they need.

### L-Carnitine — Cellular Energy for Nerve Repair

L-Carnitine is included for its ability to promote energy production, assist in nerve regeneration, and reduce nerve pain symptoms. Nerves are metabolically demanding structures. When cellular energy production drops (which happens with aging and metabolic conditions), nerve repair slows down significantly. L-carnitine shuttles fatty acids into the mitochondria — the energy factories of nerve cells — keeping the repair process running. Research has shown acetyl-L-carnitine to be particularly effective in diabetic neuropathy.

### Coenzyme Q10 (CoQ10) — Mitochondrial Repair Support

Arialief includes CoQ10 to boost cellular energy and support the healing of damaged nerves. CoQ10 levels naturally decline with age and are further depleted by statin medications — which are commonly prescribed to people who are also at risk for neuropathy. By replenishing CoQ10, the formula supports the energy infrastructure that nerve cells need to function and self-repair.

### Turmeric Extract (Curcuma Longa) — Anti-Inflammatory Support

Curcumin, the active compound in turmeric, has been extensively studied for its ability to block the NF-kB pathway — a key driver of chronic inflammation. Turmeric and ALA together help reduce inflammation around the sciatic nerve, providing relief from swelling and pain. The quality and form of the turmeric extract matters considerably since standard turmeric has poor bioavailability on its own.
